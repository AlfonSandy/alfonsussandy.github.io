Why the Waste Hub Model Works in Driyorejo

A Field-Based, Evidence-Informed Sales Perspective

Context

Driyorejo, Gresik Regency, is an area under significant environmental pressure due to dense settlements, industrial activity, and drainage systems that have exceeded their functional capacity. Field observations and academic references indicate that the core issue is not waste volume alone, but systemic leakage in upstream waste management.

Key Field Findings

  • Small plastic waste dominates river pollution
  • Drainage capacity is reduced by sedimentation and unmanaged waste
  • Community awareness exists, but daily consistency is limited
  • Waste banks and local facilities operate, but lack operational resilience

Sales Implications

Conventional sales approaches focused on short-term volume are not suitable for areas with strong social dynamics and informal systems. A sustainable approach prioritizes rhythm, reliability, and system reinforcement.

  • Selling consistency instead of one-off transactions
  • Offering guaranteed offtake rather than price competition
  • Strengthening existing local systems instead of replacing them
  • Focusing on small plastics that create latent systemic risk

Stakeholder Value

Company

More stable supply, improved material quality, and long-term revenue potential in high-risk operational areas.

Local Government

Practical legitimacy in waste management without relying on large-scale infrastructure projects.

Community

Reduced flood risk and a more orderly environment without demanding drastic behavioral change.

Conclusion

Driyorejo does not resist modern waste management. It requires a consistent operational partner capable of navigating local social and technical realities.

The Waste Hub model functions effectively as a system lock: reducing upstream leakage, stabilizing supply, and aligning environmental impact with economic value.
